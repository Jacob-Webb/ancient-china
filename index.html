Religions' Founders

By Sarah Webb 2:00 PM ET

Who started the religions? Buddhism has an interesting backstory; it was founded by Siddhartha Gautama, who lived from 563 to 483 B.C. Then there's Confucianism, which was started by the philosopher Confucius, bringing a different perspective on morality and ethics. And let's not forget Taoism, which is credited to Lao Tzu.

Ancient Wisdom Shapes China: Three Teachings That Changed a Civilization

By Sarah Webb 1:00 PM ET

Ancient China had three main religions: Buddhism, Confucianism, and Toaism.

Buddhism is a religion that is centered around the concept of pain, the suffering it causes, and how to overcome it.

Confucianism is a philosophy and a though and behavior that originated in ancient China, focusing on ethics, morality and social harmony.

Taoism si a Chinese philosophy based on the writings of Lao-tzu advocating humility and religious piety.

Each religion offers a unique way of understanding life and our place in the world. It's fascinating how these influential figures shaped their respective beliefs. Overall, the foundations of these religions reflect the diverse spiritual journeys people have taken throughout history.

What's the Meaning Of All This?

By Sarah Webb 4:00 PM ET

Final Thoughts

By Sarah Webb 5:30 PM ET

In summary, the religions of Ancient China - Buddhism, Confucianism, and Taoism - offer a rich mix of ideas about finding meaning, living ethically, and living in harmony with the world around us.

Each of these belief systems is rooted in the teachings of important figures like Siddhartha Gautama, Confucius, and Lao Tzu. They still have a big influence on the spiritual scene in East Asia and beyond. The temples and practices tied to these religions are more than just places to worship; they're also cultural hubs where communities come together and reflect on life. By diving into these philosophies, we get a glimpse of the many ways people seek understanding and connection, showing just how relevant these ancient teachings still are in our lives today.
